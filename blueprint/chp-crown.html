6 Crown & horizon

The summit, and the ranges beyond it. The summit (Thm 6.4) is reached — \(\mathsf{PA}\nvdash \gamma \), axiom-clean, via the Route-B growth path (SERIES-5). Crucially it was reached without formalizing the \(\varepsilon _0\) ordinal analysis of PA: the monument nodes below (\(\mathsf{PA}_\omega \) cut-elimination, Gentzen’s \(\mathrm{Con}(\mathsf{PA})\), the two-sided ordinal characterization) are the horizon, not summit prerequisites — Route B’s growth-rate contradiction sidesteps them. Those nodes carry no \lean binding because no declaration exists yet; they are the honest map of what a full ordinal analysis would add.

Theorem 6.1
#

Uses

  • Theorem 2.4
  • Definition 2.2

Infinitary \(\mathsf{PA}_\omega \) cut-elimination (the monument). The \(\omega \)-logic proof system for PA with the \(\omega \)-rule, an ordinal assignment \(o(d){\lt}\varepsilon _0\) to derivations, and cut-elimination that lowers the assigned ordinal — the \(\varepsilon _0\)-graded, infinitary analogue of the finitary Hauptsatz (Thm 2.4). Formalized only in Coq (Sinclaire; Bryce & Goré, arXiv:2603.00487, \(\sim \)18k lines, PA_omega.v); absent from mathlib and every other prover. \(\sim \)6–7k Lean lines, human-architected, multi-month.

Theorem 6.2
#

Uses

  • Theorem 6.1
  • Definition 3.3

\(\mathrm{TI}(\varepsilon _0)\vdash \mathrm{Con}(\mathsf{PA})\) (Gentzen’s consistency proof). From the ordinal-assigned cut-elimination, an empty-sequent PA-derivation would yield an infinite primitive-recursive \(\varepsilon _0\)-descent, forbidden by transfinite induction up to \(\varepsilon _0\); hence \(\mathrm{Con}(\mathsf{PA})\). This is the shared engine: its internalized-in-\(\mathsf{PA}\) shadow is the Route-A girder (Thm 5.11); its growth-rate contrapositive is the Route-B Wainer bound (Thm 4.5).

Theorem 6.3
#

Uses

  • Theorem 6.2
  • Definition 3.3

The proof-theoretic ordinal of PA is \(\varepsilon _0\). Both directions: \(\mathsf{PA}\) proves \(\mathrm{TI}(\alpha )\) for each \(\alpha {\lt}\varepsilon _0\), and \(\mathrm{TI}(\varepsilon _0)\) proves \(\mathrm{Con}(\mathsf{PA})\) (so PA cannot prove it). The complete ordinal analysis, of which either Goodstein route is one corollary.
