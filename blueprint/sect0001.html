1 Program overview

This is a blueprint for a proof theory / ordinal analysis of Peano Arithmetic, carried out in Lean 4. The summit of the program is the Kirby–Paris independence theorem: Peano Arithmetic does not prove that every Goodstein sequence terminates (\(\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \gamma \)). As of this writing that summit has never been formalized in any proof assistant, by any route — only the termination of Goodstein sequences is formalized (Coq hydra-battles, Isabelle/AFP Goodstein_Lambda, and Lean mathlib/lean-gallery), and abstract Gödel incompleteness is a separate lineage (Foundation, Paulson, O’Connor). A Lean proof of the independence is a first.

Everything below is organized as a mountain. Chapter 2 (Two shores) records the two bodies of already-formalized machinery this program stands between: mathlib’s ordinal apparatus and Foundation’s first-order + incompleteness apparatus. Chapter 3 (Foothills) is the reusable toolkit — the faithful encoding and faithfulness bridge (done), plus the deep gaps (Hardy/Wainer classification, arithmetic transfinite induction). Chapter 4 is the mainline route (Wainer growth rates); Chapter 5 is the banked route (internalized Gentzen cut-elimination), which carries the preserved crux-2 milestone chain. Chapter 6 is the crown: the one ordinal-analysis monument both routes ultimately require, and the sibling summits it unlocks.

The single, intrinsic obstruction shared by every route is the ordinal analysis of PA — the \(\varepsilon _0\)-bounded (infinitary) cut-elimination that yields \(\mathrm{TI}(\varepsilon _0)\vdash \mathrm{Con}(\mathsf{PA})\) (Route A face) or, equivalently, the Buchholz–Wainer bounding theorem (Route B face). It exists in no Lean library and must be originated; the only precedent is the Coq development of Bryce & Goré (arXiv:2603.00487). The lower-bound half of the climb (\(\text{goodsteinLength}\) dominates every fixed fast-growing level below \(\varepsilon _0\)) is already done and axiom-clean.
