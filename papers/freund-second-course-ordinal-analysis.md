## Abstract / scope

The advanced sequel. Where the first course climbed to **ε₀** and the ordinal analysis of **Peano arithmetic** (predicative territory), this course climbs into **impredicativity**: the **Bachmann–Howard ordinal** ϑ(ε_{Ω+1}), the method of **collapsing** (transforming uncountable proof trees into countable ones), and **operator-controlled derivations** (Buchholz). The engineered target independence result is one step up in strength from "Kruskal":

> **Corollary 7.4.** Parameter-free Π¹₁-comprehension `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻` cannot prove that `T₂(∅)` is a well partial order — i.e., it cannot prove **Harvey Friedman's extended Kruskal theorem for trees with gap condition, even for two labels.**

Reached via: the *extended* Kruskal theorem with **Friedman's gap condition** (combinatorics) ⟷ the Bachmann–Howard ordinal (notation system ϑ(ε_{Ω+1})) ⟷ the theory `ID₁` of non-iterated inductive definitions, whose proof-theoretic ordinal is the Bachmann–Howard ordinal. The course delivers a **full ordinal analysis of `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻` / `ID₁`** via collapsing. Gödel-phenomenon punchline (from the intro): since the extended Kruskal theorem is equivalent (Friedman–Robertson–Seymour) to the bounded **graph minor theorem**, the latter is unprovable in `Π¹₁-CA₀` — "one of the most spectacular manifestations of Gödel's theorems in mathematics." Note: **Goodstein's theorem is never treated** (as in the first course); the combinatorial target is gap-condition trees / graph minors, and the internal "hard" object is the Bachmann–Howard ordinal, *far above* ε₀.

Note: in the *impredicative* setting **cut elimination plays only a secondary role** — the (Fix) rule is what does the real work. |
| 6 | **Embedding and collapsing** | **Theorem 6.5 ('Embedding'):** every `ID₁`-theorem gets an infinitary derivation `H ⊢^{Ω·2+n}_{Ω+m} ψ⁺`. The keystone — **Theorem 6.7 ('Collapsing' / 'impredicative cut elimination'):** a Σ(Ω)-derivation of height `α ≺ Ω+1` controlled by `H_α` collapses to height `ϑη ≺ Ω`, `η = α+ω(β)`. This is the device that turns uncountable proof heights above `Ω` into countable ones below it — the impredicative analog of the first course's ω-exponential cut-elimination. |
| 7 | **Predicative cut elimination and the Veblen hierarchy** | Recovers the first-course cut-rank-reduction `ρ+1 → ρ` (for `ρ ≠ Ω`) and the **Veblen hierarchy** `φ(α,–)` (Def 7.6, Lemma 7.7) for cutting below limit ranks. **Theorem 7.8 ('predicative cut elimination').** **Corollary 7.2 / 7.9:** `ID₁ ⊢ ψ ⟹ H_η ⊢^{ϑη}_{ϑη} ψ⁺`. **Theorem 7.3:** the Bachmann–Howard order is **not provably well-founded in `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻`** ("sharp": the BH ordinal is the proof-theoretic ordinal of `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻`, `ID₁`, and Kripke–Platek set theory). **Corollary 7.4** (the headline independence: gap-condition Kruskal unprovable in `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻`). |

**Method, in one line:** gap-condition trees `T_N(X)` (iterated Kruskal) ⟷ Bachmann–Howard notation ϑ(ε_{Ω+1}) ⟷ conservativity `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻` over `ID₁` → embed `ID₁` into **operator-controlled infinitary derivations** (Buchholz) → **collapse** uncountable heights below `Ω` (Thm 6.7) + **predicative cut elimination** via the Veblen hierarchy (Thm 7.8) → BH ordinal not provably well-founded in `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻` (Thm 7.3) → gap-condition Kruskal / graph-minor unprovable (Cor 7.4).

## Relevance to the Goodstein-independence box

The box has two routes to "PA ⊬ Goodstein": **Route A** = the ε₀-girder / Gentzen cut-elimination route ("crux-2"), and **Route B** = the fast-growing / Hardy-hierarchy (provably-total-functions) route. Assessed against both:

**(a) Does it sit at the right strength for PA / Goodstein?** **No — it is one tier too high.** The entire course operates at the **Bachmann–Howard ordinal** and impredicative theories (`Π¹₁-CA₀⁻`, `ID₁`, Kripke–Platek). PA / Goodstein live at **ε₀**, which is *below where this course starts*. Everything genuinely new here (gap condition, ϑ-collapsing, operator control, the Veblen hierarchy, `ID₁`) is **above the ε₀ wall** the box needs and adds nothing to the PA-level argument. The PA-relevant material — infinitary derivations, the ε₀ notation system, Gentzen's `PA ⊬ TI(ε₀)`, the cut-elimination engine, the Kruskal reification — is the **first** course's content, not this one's.

**(b) Route A (cut-elimination / crux-2):** This course's collapsing (Thm 6.7) is described by Freund himself as the *impredicative* generalization of the first course's cut elimination, and it **de-emphasizes cut elimination** (§5: "cut elimination does only play a secondary role" impredicatively; the (Fix) rule dominates). So it does **not** give a cleaner or cut-elimination-*avoiding* PA route — if anything it confirms that even one level up, the well-foundedness-of-a-notation-system route is the standard wall, just collapsed differently. Operator-controlled derivations are far heavier machinery than anything PA-at-ε₀ needs; importing them would be over-engineering.

**(c) Route B (fast-growing / Hardy hierarchy):** **Absent, same as the first course.** There is **no `H_α` fast-growing hierarchy and no "f is T-provably-total iff f ≼ H_α" classification** anywhere in this course. Theorem 5.9 ('Boundedness') does extract *quantitative* content (the ordinal label bounds Σ-witness sizes), which is conceptually adjacent to provably-total-function bounds — but it is stated for the infinitary `L^Ω_ID` system at the BH level, not as the clean PA growth-rate classification the box's Route B wants. The reference for Route B remains **Fairtlough–Wainer**, not this text.

**(d) Cut-elimination as a transferable technique?** The *predicative* cut-elimination of §7 (cut-rank reduction `ρ+1 → ρ` for `ρ ≠ Ω`, Veblen-hierarchy reduction below limit ranks) is explicitly the recovered first-course method, generalized to infinite cut ranks. It is correct and clean but pitched for predicative theories of *intermediate* strength (Γ₀-ish) and above — overkill for ε₀, where the first course's plain `⊢^α_{d+1} Γ ⟹ ⊢^{ω^α}_d Γ` suffices. The collapsing of §6 has **no PA analog needed** (PA's proof heights never exceed `Ω`).

### Most reusable items if ever needed
- **Theorem 2.6** — Friedman's extended Kruskal theorem (gap condition); the canonical statement, if the box ever generalizes beyond Goodstein toward Kruskal/graph-minor independence.
- **Theorem 4.5** — `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻` conservative over `ID₁` (a clean reverse-math/proof-theory fact, BH-level).
- **§5–6 (operator control + collapsing)** — Buchholz's modern impredicative apparatus, *only* relevant if the project's scope ever climbs from ε₀ to the Bachmann–Howard ordinal.

## Verdict (2–3 sentences)

**The second course adds essentially nothing route-relevant beyond the first for a PA/Goodstein box: it operates entirely *above* the ε₀ wall** (Bachmann–Howard ordinal, impredicative `Π¹₁-CA₀⁻`/`ID₁`, gap-condition trees / graph minors), so its genuinely new machinery — ϑ-collapsing, operator-controlled derivations, the Veblen hierarchy — is over-strength for both routes and never touches Goodstein. On **Route A** it merely confirms the well-foundedness/cut-elimination route at a higher tier (and even de-emphasizes cut elimination impredicatively), and on **Route B** it again contains **no fast-growing-hierarchy or provably-total-function classification** (Fairtlough–Wainer remains the reference). Keep the *first* course as the on-target ε₀-girder text; this sequel is a "deeper/reference" companion only, useful solely if the project's ambitions ever scale past ε₀.
