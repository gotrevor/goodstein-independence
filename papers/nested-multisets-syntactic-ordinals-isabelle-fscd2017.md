## Abstract

A formalized Isabelle/HOL library of results about **finite nested multisets**. The central object is the **nested multiset order** (Dershowitz–Manna's generalization of the multiset order, which raises the order's ordinal type from `ω^α` to `ε₀ = ω^{ω^{ω^…}}`), with machine-checked proofs that it **preserves well-foundedness and totality (linearity)**. Restricting nested multisets to drop the leaf constructor gives **hereditary multisets**, which provide a convenient **syntactic representation of ordinals below ε₀** — multiset multiplicities are the Cantor-normal-form coefficients `cᵢ`, nesting depth encodes the `ω`-exponents. Both nested and hereditary multisets are defined as ordinary **inductive datatypes** (possible because `multiset` is a *bounded natural functor*, permitting recursion through it — a flexibility absent in other provers). The library also formalizes the non-standard **signed (hybrid) multisets** for ordinals with negative coefficients, and uses **Hessenberg (natural) ordinal operations** throughout. Two applications: a formalized **Goodstein's theorem** and the **decidability of unary PCF**.

## Key content

### Nested multiset order
- **Datatype**: `'a nmultiset = Elem 'a | MSet (('a nmultiset) multiset)` — a leaf carrying a base element, or a finite multiset of nested multisets. The Dershowitz–Manna **nested** order `≪*` (overloaded `<`) is defined by mutual recursion on two nested multisets, lifting the base order with the DM extension `ext_DM` at the `MSet`/`MSet` case.
- Recursion *through* the non-datatype `multiset` works because `(multiset, image, set, ℵ₀)` is a **bounded natural functor (BNF)**; `image` is the functorial action (≈ `map`), `set` the natural transformation. The authors stress HOL+BNF gives **simpler definitions and proofs** than the quotient-of-lists (Lean/HOL), setoid (Agda/Coq), or `'a → nat` approaches.
- The hard theorems are **transitivity** (via equivalence of `ext_DM` with the `mult` closure on transitive relations) and **well-foundedness** (auxiliary lemma: `<` is well-founded on the set of nested multisets of *fixed depth* `i`, by induction on `i`; then lexicographically compare depth, breaking ties with the nested order). Each closure property (preorder / partial order / total order / wellorder) is a **type-class instantiation**, which automatically delivers a wealth of reusable lemmas.

### Syntactic ε₀ ordinals as hereditary multisets
- **Datatype**: `hmultiset = HMSet (hmultiset multiset)` — nested multisets with no `Elem` (a "multiset-based hereditarily-finite set"). Embeds into `'a nmultiset` via Isabelle's **Lifting/Transfer** tools, so order infrastructure lifts down for free.
- **The ordinal correspondence** (the reusable idea): a CNF ordinal `α = ω^{α₁}·c₁ + ⋯ + ω^{αₙ}·cₙ` (with `α₁ > ⋯ > αₙ`) is exactly the hereditary multiset `{α₁,…,α₁, …, αₙ,…,αₙ}` (each `αᵢ` repeated `cᵢ` times). Concretely: `{} = 0`, `{0} = ω⁰ = 1`, `{0,0,0} = 3`, `{1} = ω`, `{ω} = ω^ω`. **The order `<` on hereditary multisets coincides definitionally with the ordinal `<`**, and **multiset subtraction is truncating ordinal subtraction** (`1−3 = 0`, `ω−3 = ω`, `ω²+3−ω = ω²+3`).
- **Hessenberg (natural) operations**: `+` is Hessenberg addition (commutative, unlike `1+ω = ω ≠ ω+1`); the Hessenberg product is the cartesian product of the operand multisets with `+` on exponents. These commute/associate/distribute, share many properties with ℕ, and are easier to automate — they replace the non-commutative ordinal `+`/`·` throughout. A `head_ω` "degree" function and a `hmset_pair_decompose_less` lemma support clean ordinal comparisons.
- **Signed (hybrid) multisets** `'a zmultiset` (quotient of pairs `(P,N)` ≈ `P − N`, à la `int` from `(m,n)`) and `zhmultiset` extend this to ordinals with *negative* coefficients (e.g. `ω² − 2ω + 1`), motivated by a transfinite Knuth–Bendix order formalization; subtraction is so ill-behaved that multiplication does **not** distribute over it.

### The Isabelle Goodstein formalization (§8)
- A number is in **hereditary base `b`** when written with the exponents themselves recursively in hereditary base `b` — *the* point being that "hereditary base" notation **is** a hereditary multiset / syntactic ordinal (e.g. `2·ω²+2·ω+2` ↔ `2·3²+2·3+2` in hereditary base 3).
- Defines `encode : nat → nat → hmultiset` and `decode : nat → hmultiset → nat` in a context fixing `base ≥ 2`, converting between naturals and their ε₀-ordinal images. The Goodstein sequence `goodstein (i+1) = decode_{i+3} 0 (encode_{i+2} 0 (goodstein i)) − 1`.
- **Termination** is the one-line payoff: thanks to well-foundedness of `<` on syntactic ordinals, `goodstein_step` shows `Eᵢ > E_{i+1}` (the associated ordinals strictly descend), so the sequence hits 0. The whole development is **≈ 580 lines**; "reasoning about ordinals was fairly comfortable." The base-bump-then-subtract maps to: re-encode (no ordinal change) then `decode … − 1` strictly decreases the ordinal.
- Also: a Hydra-battle termination proof (§ Example 2, via an `encode` measure into the ordinals) and McCarthy's 91-function termination via the plain multiset order.

## Relevance

This box internalizes the *opposite* of the Isabelle setup: it carries ε₀ ordinals **twice** — at the meta level as mathlib's `ONote` (Cantor normal form), and **inside a model `V ⊧ₘ* 𝗜𝚺₁`** as hand-rolled HFS-pair codes (`InternalONote`: `0 ↦ 0`, `oadd e n r ↦ ⟪⟪ec,n⟫,rc⟫+1`) with explicit `𝚺₀/𝚺₁`-definability obligations on every projection, a CNF comparison `icmp`, and Hessenberg natural addition (`InternalNadd`/`NADD.lean`). The hard wall is order-reflection of an `≺`-descent inside `M` (lap-37), not termination per se.

**Does the nested-multiset encoding offer a cleaner encoding for the box?** *Mostly no, with one design lesson to steal.*

1. **For the in-model ordinal layer: no.** The box's binding constraint is **arithmetic definability** — every ordinal operation must be `𝚺₀/𝚺₁`-definable as a course-of-values recursion on the *code value* of an HFS object, so it can run inside `V ⊧ₘ* 𝗜𝚺₁`. A `multiset`-of-`multiset` datatype is exactly the BNF-recursion-through-a-non-datatype trick that **HOL can do and the model-internal arithmetization cannot**: there is no `multiset` type inside `M`, only HFS codes and bounded recursion. The box already *is* using the hereditary-multiset idea — its `oadd` CNF codes are a flattened, totally-ordered-exponent normal form of the same `{α₁,…,αₙ}` content — but committed (correctly) to a CNF list rather than an unordered multiset because **CNF gives a canonical representative and a decidable subterm bound**, which the box's `ocExp/ocCoeff/ocTail < code` lemmas need for the recursion to terminate. An unordered multiset would *add* a quotient/normalization burden inside `M`, not remove one.

2. **For a hypothetical *meta-level* Lean ε₀/Hardy build: also no, but for a different reason.** mathlib already ships `ONote` (CNF) + `ONote.fastGrowing`, and the box's `wip/FastGrowing.lean` is building the missing monotonicity/domination lemmas on *that*. mathlib has **no** hereditary-multiset ordinal type, so adopting the nested-multiset representation would mean reimplementing the ε₀ order from scratch and re-proving well-foundedness — strictly more work than leaning on `ONote`. The Isabelle win ("ordinals are just an inductive datatype, well-foundedness falls out of a depth induction") is **already cashed by `ONote` in Lean** via `ONote.repr` into `Ordinal` + `WellFoundedLT`.

3. **The reusable formalization-design lesson — Hessenberg-everywhere.** The paper's sharpest transferable choice is using **Hessenberg (natural) `+`/`·`** instead of ordinal `+`/`·` *throughout*, precisely because they are commutative/associative/distributive and "share many properties with ℕ, so they are easier to automate." The box already mirrors this (its in-model `InternalNadd`/`NADD` is natural addition), which is independent corroboration that this is the right call — and a cue to keep the meta-level Hardy/embedding arithmetic on Hessenberg operations too (the `EmbeddingBound` ω-rule supremum `⨆ₙ α(n)+1` wants the well-behaved natural-sum algebra). Two more lessons worth noting: (a) **closure properties as type-class instantiations** (preorder→…→wellorder), which the box partly gets for free from mathlib's `Ordinal` order instances but does *not* get for its in-model codes (those need bespoke `icmp`/`evalNat_lt_iff` lemmas — a reminder that the model-internal layer pays a tax HOL avoids); (b) the **depth-stratified well-foundedness** proof technique (well-founded on fixed depth `i`, then lex on depth) is a clean pattern if the box ever needs in-model well-foundedness of `≺` without routing through mathlib's external `Ordinal`.

**Net**: this paper is a **cross-prover sanity check and design corroborator** (Hessenberg ops, hereditary-base ↔ syntactic-ordinal identity, "termination is one strict-descent line once well-foundedness is in hand"), not a source of a cleaner encoding to port. Its 580-line Goodstein proof is the *easy* (meta-level, termination-only) target; the box's hard problem — **PA's inability to certify ε₀-well-foundedness, internalized** — is exactly the content this comfortable HOL development assumes away.
