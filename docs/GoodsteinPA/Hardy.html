      The CNF norm and structural comparison helpers (toward general Bachmann reachability) #

      The general index-domination needs a budget condition: the diagonal argument x must be large enough that the fundamental-sequence descent of β actually passes through α. The right "size of α" is its CNF norm: the largest finite number (coefficient or finite tail) appearing anywhere in α's Cantor normal form.

      def GoodsteinPA.FastGrowing.norm :
      ONote → ℕ

      CNF norm of a notation: the maximum finite coefficient appearing anywhere in its Cantor normal form (recursively through exponents and tails). norm 0 = 0, norm (ω^e·n + a) = max (norm e) (max n (norm a)).
